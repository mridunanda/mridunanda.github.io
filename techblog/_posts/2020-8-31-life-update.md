Wow. I really have not been on here for a while. I'm going to try to be more consistent with my updates including personal updates, school updates, and cool things I learn about. Lol, we will see how long this lasts. Anyways there is alot of material to cover, so let's get started.

January 2020: Ah the new year! I have no idea what is yet to come. So naive. It was my brother's birthday, so I enjoyed being at home and celebrating with him. Kobe died, and everyone was sad. I actually didn't really know anything about Kobe other than he was a basketball player and people yelled his name when shooting stuff into a trashcan. So when the news broke, I did my research. He seemed like a super passionate guy, and I too felt sad about his passing.

Feburary: School, as usual, ramps up quickly. This semester I took CS 161 (Operating Systems), Math 154 (Introduction to Probability), Phil 17 (Feminism, I don't actually remember the formal course name lol), and CS 91R (which I used to do my research with Prof. Mickens). I was really nervous about 161, since I had heard horror stories about the number of hours the classes taks (over 30 hrs/week in some years). 

March: School is in full swing. My best friend from high school visits me at Harvard and we have a small reunion. We spend the majority of the weekend eating at restaurants and chilling. However, I, as usual, freak out in the time that I am not working. I force my friends to study at one point and use that time to knock off my feminism essay. Then I spend the rest of the weekend balancing research and probablity homework. Spring break is coming up so I figure I will start the next 161 pset then, instead of trying to cram it in now. Overall, I feel happy to spend time with my friends that I don't see too often, but I feel drained by all the work. School is really picking up and I need the vacation. 

The week before spring break: My roommate warns me about us being potentially sent home due to the pandemic. She had insider information because she was going to participate in the NCAA tournament. I find the possiblity intriguing, but don't think much of it. 

Two days before spring break: The sun is out, the sky is blue. Everything is warm, spring is finally here! Cambridge is beautiful. I wear a tshirt, shorts, and flip flops. I'm in such a good mood, I stop by for an iced coffee (iced because it is finally warm outside!!) on my way to OS class. I strut into class 15 minutes late without a care in the world. We are going home in 2 days, and I am so excited to see my family and boyfriend at home. Lecture was moderately interesting and then I head out to catch up with my best friend. Me, my friend, and our professor meet up outside our professor's office to chat (I usually have a research meeting with the professor after class). Me and my friend joke about my roommates comments and declare "It's time for summer baby!". 

Day before spring break: I wake up and my phone is flooded with messages. Quickly, I check my email. I realize that my roommates intel was indeed correct. I read the email a couple times and it seems like they are sending us all home within the next 5 days. No "this is temporary" or "You will be back in a month or so". This is permenant, like till the end of the semester. My heart sinks. I didn't realize this would be a permenant type of situation. I get out of bed and start talking to my suitemates. Everyone is in disbelief. Let me summarize the crazines that ensues in the next 5ish days. 

- Everyone makes a mad scramble for college boxes in order to pack their stuff out. Boxes run out within minutes each time the building manager notifies that a new shipment has arrived. I got my pick of the boxes. 
- I realize I will have to postpone my ticket so that I have time to pack my stuff up. I postpone it till Saturday so I can spend the maximum amount of reamaining time with my friends.
- People are raging on campus, throwing parties left and right. I judge them silently for being irresponsible. 
- Seniors are devastated. I feel really bad for them :( I had a 3 good friends who were seniors. One of them I get to hang out with (see below bullet point). The other two, not so much since they were caught up in thing that seniors do TM. 
- My and two friends go hunting for waffles, grille food, and then finally settle on nachos at Felipes. We come back to my room take some shots (that I had to get rid of) and practice dancing to renegade on TikTok.
- One of my blockmates was graduating early, so we get together and hang out with him before it is time for him to leave. We have a fun hang out in Mather yard with the boys playing cornhole and generally vibing. Everything had an end of the world vibe. 
- My blockmates start to freak out about the virus. Their paranoia rubs off on my, and I prepone my postponed ticket. Now I am scheduled to leave on Friday. Luckily the airlines were waiving all fees, so it wasn't too big a deal minus the extremely long phone wait.
- I binge watch trash TV with my best friend. We watch the shows at 3x speed so they are especially comical. We order insomnia cookies and milk for this final hurrah. The reality is finally setting in, and we have a someewhat sad goodbye.
